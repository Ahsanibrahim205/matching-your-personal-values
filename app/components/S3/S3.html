  • Now that you have identified ten values that are very important to you, put them in order of personal priority by using the Guide for Prioritising Values.

Guide for Prioritising Values

  • List the values you want to put in order
    1. Number each value
  • Prepare a numbers grid
    1. Take one value and pair it with each of the others by writing the value’s number beside it in a column, as in Column A below. Repeat this with each succeeding value, dropping down one space each time.
    • A B C D E F G H I
    1 2 - - - - - - - -
    1 3 2 3 - - - - - - -

    1 10 2 10 3 10 4 10 5 10 6 10 7 10 8 10 9 10

    You can build a numbers grid for as many values as you need.

  • Make simple decisions
    1. Go to Column A and consider each pair by itself. The first pair is 1 and 2, so ask yourself, “If I could have only one of these two values, which one would I prefer?” Whatever the answer, you circle the number corresponding to your choice.
    2. Then you go on to the next pair in Column A: 1 3, and again ask yourself, “If I could have only one of these two values, which one would I prefer?”. Again, circle the number that corresponds to your choice.
    3. Go on and make your choices for each succeeding pair in Column A, then Columns, B, C, D, E, F, G, H and I. For each pair of numbers in each column you make a choice by repeatedly asking yourself: “If only I could have one of these two, which would I prefer?”
  • Total up the number of times each number was circled
  • Rearrange the numbers in order of preference
    1. Your list is now prioritised.
  • The final step is to examine the list and see whether your intuition agrees. If it disagrees, change the list. If you have a tie between values, you can leave them tied or use intuition to choose one over the other. This technique is designed to serve you, not you it. You and your intuition are the final judge. If you disagree with the final ordering of values, change it.
